Gen 5 Your 2x Great-Grandparents 1896 – 1989
Andrew “Pop” Gemeny
Nov 16, 1896 – Mar 30, 1989
St. Michaels, MD → Brandywine, MD
Myrtelle M. Gordon
Dec 13, 1896 – Jul 23, 1967
Washington, DC → College Park, MD

“Pop” Gemeny was the family storyteller. In the 1970s, he sat down with a tape recorder and told stories about the family — stories that had been passed down for generations. Those recordings were later digitized and preserved.

Notable

Pop and Myrtelle married on May 18, 1929 at an Episcopal Church in New York. Pop lived to 92 years old. After Myrtelle’s death, he married Lucy Belle Pittman — whose green tomato pickle recipe is preserved in the family archive.

🎤 Pop’s oral history recordings from the 1970s are one of the most precious family artifacts.
♆ Pop was a Freemason. He lived at “Gemeny Farm” in Prince George’s County, Maryland.

Andrew was a Civil War soldier from Virginia and a carpenter by trade. He grew up near Pleasant View in Kinsale, then moved to Royal Oak on Maryland’s Eastern Shore.

Nora was widowed at age 44 when Andrew died in 1882. She survived him by 47 years, dying in 1929 at age 92 at Swain Farm in Talbot County. Two of their children — Lillie May and Wilbur Fisk — died as infants.

Gen 2 Your 5x Great-Grandparents 1791 – 1876
John Henry Gemeny II
Mar 25, 1791 – Feb 8, 1850
Massachusetts → Alexandria → Kinsale, VA
Matilda “Milly” Figg
Dec 23, 1796 – May 6, 1876
Virginia → Kinsale, VA

John II is the man from whom every living Gemeny descends. He built Pleasant View in Kinsale, Virginia — a beautiful home on a hill overlooking the Potomac River. He and Matilda raised nine children there.

The Cupboard Legend

As a boy, John II was meant to go to sea with his father. His mother Naomi hid him in a cupboard on the day his father sailed away and was never heard from again. If she hadn’t hidden him, the Gemeny line would have ended.

The General Harrison Quilt

A beautiful Baltimore Album Quilt was given to John and Matilda with their initials to celebrate their wedding. It has been in the family for over 200 years.

⚔ John II’s brother Benjamin was imprisoned as a Union spy during the Civil War. Benjamin’s wife Mary wrote a memoir about it in 1913.
Gen 1 Your 6x Great-Grandparents 1769 – c. 1804
John “The Sea Captain” Gemeny
Nov 5, 1769 – c. 1804
Salem, MA → Philadelphia → Lost at sea
Naomi Tewksbury
1770 – ?
New Hampshire → Salem → Philadelphia → Baltimore

John was a ship’s captain who sailed merchant vessels out of Philadelphia. He was a twin — born the same day as his sister Mary. Around 1804, he sailed on a voyage and was never heard from again.

The Name Change

Naomi is credited with changing the family name from Gemini (or Jemminy) to Gemeny. She settled in Baltimore after John’s disappearance and ran a rooming house.

🚢 John’s burial record simply says: “Lost at sea per family bible.”
Gen 0 The Founding Ancestor c. 1730 – 1776
Anthony Gemini
c. 1730 – 1776
Unknown origin → Salem, Massachusetts
Catherine Richards
Apr 7, 1730 – ?
Salem, Massachusetts

This is where it all begins. Anthony Gemini — a sailor — arrived in North America around 1755. Nobody knows exactly where he came from. The family always assumed Italy, but a 2002 research essay suggested possible English roots through Thomas Gemini of Louvain, a famous engraver.

Anthony married Catherine Richards on October 17, 1756 in Salem, Massachusetts. He died in 1776 — the year America declared independence.

The Founding Marriage

October 17, 1756 — Salem, Massachusetts. Every Gemeny in this country traces back to this moment.
